Ramen is delicous. Here is how to make it at home without spending hours in the kitchen. This recipe in particular is for tsukemen, a ramen dish served with cold noodles. Add some more filler nonsense....

Ingredients

Here is a list of what you will need:

  • 2 packages of dry ramen noodles
  • 1 pound thin sliced pork belly or shabu shabu
  • 2 cups chinese chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp Sake
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 inch piece ginger root
  • 1 tsp dried chili cut into rings
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 negi or long green onion
  • 1 handful dried shiitake mushroom
  • 1 bunch shimeji mushroom
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

For toppings:

  • 1 soft boiled egg
  • spring onion leaves
  • Bonito flakes
    •
  • Grated garlic

Instructions

  1. Cut pork belly in to 2 inch strips. Using a wooden spoon, flatten the garlic clove and the ginger root and slice the whites of the green onion on a strong bias. Reserve green section.
  1. Add pork belly slices to medium sized pot over medium heat until fat begins to render.
  1. Add the saki and chili rings and continue rendering until there is a nice base of fat.
  1. Add the chicken stock, garlic, ginger, onion greens, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  1. Strain out onion, garlic and ginger, as well as any foam that has risen to the top.
  1. Add cut green onion, both mushrooms, and soy sauce. Return to a boil.
  1. At a boil, return to a simmer until onions begin to wilt slightly. Continue for 5 mins.
  1. Test broth for salt and spice level. If (acceptable) {add:sesame oil and vinegar} else {adjust first}. Remove from heat.
  1. For noodles, use fresh frozen. Boil for no more than 3 mins. Immediately strain and rinse under cold water to stop cooking process. Squeeze dry.
  1. Cook the egg however you want man, I don't care
  1. Serve ramen separate from noodles. Top with grated garlic, bonito, chili, or togarashi. Remember to dip your noodles!
